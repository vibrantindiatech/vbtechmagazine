"Digital is Not a Strategy—It's the Market."

A Conversation with Rajesh Verma, CEO of Apex Home Solutions

Sept 29, 2025 By: Anita Sharma

SHARE

Rajesh Verma, the visionary behind Apex Home Solutions, is redefining the houseware and appliance landscape in India. We sat down with him to discuss the shift from traditional manufacturing to a data-driven, consumer-centric model and the strategic pivots needed to thrive in the new decade.

VIM: The pace of digital adoption has accelerated. How has Apex Home Solutions adjusted its supply chain and B2B approach to meet this change?

Rajesh Verma: It’s been transformational, not just an adjustment. We realized early that digital is not a strategy—it's the market itself. Our B2B distributors now demand real-time inventory visibility and just-in-time delivery. We invested heavily in cloud-based logistics platforms and decentralized our warehousing. This allows us to serve Tier 2 and Tier 3 cities with the same efficiency as Mumbai or Delhi. The B2B relationship is now a partnership based on shared data, reducing risk for everyone.

"The biggest mistake a manufacturer can make today is designing a product without first seeing the user's kitchen."

Rajesh Verma, CEO

VIM: Let's talk product. What is the biggest design pivot Apex has made in the last two years, driven by consumer feedback from the Indian market?

Rajesh Verma: It's the move toward minimalist, modular design. Indian kitchens, particularly in apartments, are space-constrained. Consumers demanded appliances that serve multiple functions and are easy to store. For example, our new modular induction cooktop system can be broken down into three independent units or stacked seamlessly. We found that performance and quality are non-negotiable, but if the product doesn't fit the physical constraints of their home, it simply won't sell. This shift requires us to rethink everything, from component sourcing to packaging size.

VIM: The B2B trade show model is evolving. What role do physical trade events like the Vibrant India Home Expo still play in your strategy compared to digital platforms?

Rajesh Verma: Trade shows remain absolutely crucial, but their function has changed. Digital platforms are for efficient reordering and low-touch transactions. Trade events are for trust-building, large-volume contracts, and tactile product experience. You cannot truly appreciate the finish of our premium stainless steel line or the interface of a new smart appliance without seeing and touching it. Furthermore, it's the perfect environment for us to conduct high-level strategic discussions with national distributors, which often lead to exclusive partnership deals you simply can't close over a Zoom call. It’s where relationships are solidified.

Image: Apex's new automated production line, driving 20% efficiency gains.

VIM: Looking ahead 3-5 years, what is the single biggest opportunity for the Indian houseware sector, and what is the biggest risk?

Rajesh Verma: The biggest opportunity is the Rise of the Affluent Tier 2 Consumer. Their spending power is growing faster than metro cities, and they demand premium, branded, quality products. Companies that invest in local distribution and regional marketing will capture this segment. The biggest risk, ironically, is also the market: Global Volatility. Unpredictable raw material prices (metals, plastics) and international trade dynamics require us to build greater domestic resilience and hedge against currency fluctuations. It's a challenging but highly rewarding environment.

About Rajesh Verma

Rajesh Verma is the CEO and Chairman of Apex Home Solutions, a leader in premium stainless steel kitchenware and smart home appliances in India. With over two decades in the industry, he is an advocate for sustainable manufacturing and data-driven supply chain management.

Recipient of the 2023 India Manufacturing Excellence Award.

Based in Mumbai, leading a team of 4,000+ employees.
